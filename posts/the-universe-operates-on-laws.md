## The delivery guy
Back in university, I used to worry about the concept of paying salaries. What happens if I can't make payroll? The idea terrified me. I was too young to understand that the answer.

My thinking has since changed tremendously. I firmly believe that it is God's responsibility to provide for people. I am simply a vessel. I don't put food on my employees' table. I'm just the delivery guy.

This belief has served me well. We hire without worry now because I know our fates are tied. God has to provide for my team, and our revenue growth is an indirect consequence of that. If I have to let someone go, it simply means I wasn't the right delivery guy for them. Someone else is.

## Generosity and wealth
When I look at the people around me who've done well, I see a recurring pattern. The ones who give generously tend to receive abundantly. Is it their wealth that enables their generosity? Or is it their generosity that sustains their wealth?

I believe the latter. The effect is the cause. This isn't just a feel-good statement. I've seen it play out in my own family and across every successful circle I've been part of. The people who give freely don't seem to run out. The people who hoard always seem to be running low.

Money spent on charity returns to you manifold. I can't prove this with a formula, but I've observed it too many times to dismiss it.

## Cause and effect blur
There's a concept in quantum physics that has always fascinated me. When two particles are entangled, observing the spin of one instantly determines the spin of the other, regardless of the distance between them. Faster than light. The cause and the effect are not sequential. They are connected in a way that defies our linear understanding of time.

There's a similar idea in Norse myth. Odin foresaw that Loki would rebel. He saw the future and tried to prevent it. But here's the twist: it was Odin's prophecy itself that made Loki rebel. The moment Odin treated Loki as the future traitor, he set in motion the very betrayal he was trying to avoid. The effect became the cause. The future reached back and shaped the present.

You see it in markets too. When Elon says Doge will go up, people buy. Doge goes up. His statement didn't predict the future. It created it. The effect was the cause.

I'm no physicist. But this idea mirrors something I observe constantly in life. The future seems to shape the present just as much as the present shapes the future. Decisions I've made today were somehow set up by events that hadn't happened yet. It sounds irrational, but the pattern keeps showing up.

## Sensitivity to initial conditions
Small actions can have an enormous impact on outcomes. Complex systems are highly sensitive to their starting conditions. The flap of a butterfly's wings in Mexico can cause a storm in China. This is not poetry. It is a well-studied principle in chaos theory.

The laws of physics and the wisdom our ancestors left behind all point to this. Everything is connected. I sometimes observe patterns at the subatomic level and find myself baffled to see those same patterns playing out between complex beings like humans. Atoms behave in probabilistic ways. So do people. Markets. Relationships. Entire civilizations.

Nature follows laws. And if we pay close enough attention, we can use those laws to our advantage. Not by trying to control outcomes, but by understanding which initial conditions lead to the results we want. You don't control the storm. You choose where to plant the seed.

## The unseen system
I think most people go through life believing that the world is random and chaotic. I used to think that too. But the more I observe, the more I see structure underneath the noise. Not a rigid, deterministic structure, but a probabilistic one. Patterns that repeat across scales. Rules that govern both particles and people.

The universe is not indifferent. It operates on laws. Some of them we understand. Most of them we don't. But they're there, running in the background, whether we acknowledge them or not. The sooner you start paying attention to them, the sooner things start making sense.

I don't claim to understand how it all works. But I know enough to trust it.
